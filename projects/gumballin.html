GUMBALLIN

A mechatronics system created using Lego Mindstorms NXT.

Repository
About

Gumballin is a mechatronics system in the form of a gumball machine developed by myself and three others as our first year summative design project. This project was created as a solution to the following problem: current commericial gumball machines don't provide the user with enough freedom in terms of dispensing options (i.e., they only dispense a random coloured gumball one at a time). Our proposed solution would allow the user to choose how many gumballs and even what colour of gumball they would like. The overview of the system's workflow is broken down into four phases: accepting, sorting, user input, dispensing. To execute our solution, focus would be put onto the user input and dispensing phases, while keeping in mind not to compromise the remaining phases.

The core functionality of our design was provided by the Lego NXT Brick (32-bit microprocessor/display) and multiple sensors from the Lego Mindstorms NXT Robotics Kit, in addition to code developed in RobotC specifically for the system. The physical structure of our system was created by using the TETRIX Robotics Kit, custom 3D printed parts, and whatever regular Lego pieces we could find.

Approach

In terms of design, several sketches and mockups were created and approved by a TA before starting the final product. Once work on the end product commenced, the group was divided into two sub-teams. One was in charge of the physical aspects of the system (i.e., construction of the frame, integration of sensors, positioning of 3D printed parts), while the other was in charge of the software development (i.e., general system workflow, sensor functionality, debugging). The team came together once again in order to do trial runs and ensure that any issues present were solved before doing the demo.
